About Agtalin Shrine

The Agtalin Shrine is a place of religious significance and a symbol of the history and culture of Pilar, Capiz. The shrine is dedicated to the Virgin Mary and serves as a focal point for both locals and visitors, showcasing the rich heritage of the town.

History of Agtalin Shrine

The Agtalin Shrine was constructed during the early Spanish colonial period as a religious landmark. Over the centuries, it has been a central point for the local community's faith and traditions. The shrine continues to hold significant spiritual value and is a point of pride for the people of Pilar.

Location

The Agtalin Shrine is located in Pilar, Capiz, situated on the island of Panay in the Philippines. It is easily accessible by road from Roxas City and other neighboring towns.
